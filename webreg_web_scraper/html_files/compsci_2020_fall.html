Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences

Donald Bren School of Information and Computer Sciences comments:

For ICS course restriction information, visit the ICS Course Updates webpage: http://www.ics.uci.edu/ugrad/sao/Course_updates.php

Note that it is incumbent on the student to check the restriciton status and seating availability of a course before registering or attempting to add or change their discussion/lab section enrollment.

DEADLINE TO ADD/DROP/CHANGE GRADE OPTION FOR ALL SCHOOL OF ICS COURSES:

5:00pm on Friday of Week 2: Early deadline to add/drop/change grade option for any course specifically noted with a comment in the Schedule of Classes. Permission from the instructor and an authorization code will be required after Week 1 to add or drop such courses.

5:00pm on Friday of Week 2: Deadline add/drop/change grade option for all courses through WebReg. The Bren School of ICS STRICTLY adheres to and enforces this deadline for both undergraduate and graduate courses.

ADDING, DROPPING, OR CHANGING GRADE OPTION AFTER WEEK 2: The deadline to add/drop/change grade option is 5:00pm on Friday, October 16, 2020. After this deadline, students must submit an Enrollment Exception request online through StudentAccess. Exception requests will only be approved for extenuating and documented circumstances outside of the student's control.

COURSE PREREQUISITES ARE STRICTLY ENFORCED. The School WILL DROP students who do not meet the required prerequisites from courses. Note that meeting the prerequisites for a course does not guarantee a seat in that course.

GRADUATE ENROLLMENT IN UNDERGRADUATE COURSES: Priority for seating in undergraduate level courses is given first to undergraduate students. Graduate students seeking to enroll in an ICS undergraduate level course must submit a request to ICS Student Affairs. Note that approval is contingent on seating availability, and unauthorized students found enrolled in undergraduate courses WILL BE DROPPED by ICS Student Affairs.

UNDERGRADUATE ENROLLMENT IN GRADUATE COURSES: Priority for seating in graduate level courses is given first to graduate students. Undergraduate students seeking to enroll in an ICS graduate level course must obtain written permission from the instructor before submitting a request for authorization to ICS Student Affairs.. Students should be aware that graduate courses taken as an undergraduate will not count toward a graduate degree program.

DCE CONCURRENT ENROLLMENT:Students enrolling in courses through Division of Continuing Education Programs may only request courses that show as "OPEN" in the Schedule of Classes. Approval of requests is contingent on seating availability and prerequisite eligibility.
